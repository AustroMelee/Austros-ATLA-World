### 📖 Overview

The Hermit Crab is a standard crustacean found in the Air Temples and surrounding areas. These fascinating creatures are known for their unique behavior of finding and inhabiting empty shells, which they carry on their backs for protection. As they grow, hermit crabs must find larger shells to accommodate their increasing size.

### ✨ Notable Examples

- **Temple Hermit Crabs:** Various hermit crabs that inhabit the Air Temples, particularly in areas where they can find suitable shells and food sources.

### 🌍 Habitat & Distribution

Hermit crabs are commonly found in coastal areas, forests, and around the Air Temples where they can find empty shells and suitable food sources. They prefer humid environments and areas with access to both land and water.

### 🔗 Cultural Significance

Hermit crabs represent the importance of adaptation and finding one's place in the world. Their behavior of seeking and inhabiting shells serves as a metaphor for finding shelter and protection in life. They also represent the connection between land and sea environments.

### 🎭 Behavior & Characteristics

Hermit crabs are known for their unique shell-carrying behavior. They are omnivorous, eating both plant and animal matter. They are highly adaptable creatures that can live in various environments, from coastal areas to forests. Their soft abdomen is protected by the shell they carry.
