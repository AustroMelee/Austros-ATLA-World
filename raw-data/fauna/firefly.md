### 📖 Overview

The Firefly is a common bioluminescent flying insect found throughout the Air Temples and surrounding areas. These gentle creatures are known for their ability to produce light through bioluminescence, creating beautiful displays in the night sky. Fireflies are often associated with the peaceful and spiritual nature of the Air Nomads.

### ✨ Notable Examples

- **Temple Fireflies:** Various fireflies that inhabit the Air Temples, creating natural lighting displays during evening hours.

### 🌍 Habitat & Distribution

Fireflies are commonly found in forested areas, meadows, and around the Air Temples where they can find suitable vegetation and moisture. They are most active during warm summer evenings and are particularly abundant in areas with rich plant life.

### 🔗 Cultural Significance

Fireflies represent the gentle, peaceful nature of the Air Nomads and their connection to natural beauty. Their bioluminescent displays are often seen as spiritual manifestations, reminding people of the beauty and wonder of the natural world. They symbolize hope and light in darkness.

### 🎭 Behavior & Characteristics

Fireflies are nocturnal insects that use bioluminescence to communicate and attract mates. They are gentle creatures that pose no threat to humans or other animals. They feed primarily on nectar and other plant materials, and their light displays are synchronized in some species to create spectacular natural shows.
